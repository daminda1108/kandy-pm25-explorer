How it works & integrity

← Back to explorer

The Kandy PM2.5 Explorer reconstructs a 1 km, hourly fine-particulate field over the Kandy valley for 2019–2023. Kandy has no public real-time air-quality monitor, so the model borrows structure from satellites, reanalysis meteorology, terrain physics, and validated ground data from analogue cities. This page is deliberately explicit about what is measured, what is anchored, and what is a physically-motivated scenario — so a polished map never over-claims.

The model in one equation

PM(x, y, t) = B(t) + [ T(t) − B(t) ] · Plocal(x, y, t)
  • T(t) — the basin-mean level for each hour, from a gradient-boosted model on exogenous drivers (ERA5 meteorology, GEOS-CF and CAMS chemistry, satellite AOD and NO₂, solar radiation), re-anchored each year to the satellite-derived annual mean and sharpened to the observed diurnal and seasonal swing.
  • B(t) — the regional/transboundary background that arrives over the region regardless of local emissions (a rural satellite floor shaped by the seasonal cycle and conditioned on air-mass origin).
  • Plocal(x, y, t) — the within-valley spatial pattern (mean 1): a traffic-emission surface modulated by boundary-layer confinement and mass-consistent WindNinja terrain winds (channelling, night-time katabatic drainage, day-time mixing).

Because the field is anchored — its basin mean each hour equals T(t) — the entire animation is reconstructed in your browser from a few small arrays. Nothing is fetched from a server as you scrub; the physics was computed once, offline.

Why the explorer stops at 2023

The level is anchored to the satellite-derived annual PM2.5 product, whose latest release covers up to 2023. We deliberately do not extrapolate the annual level into 2024 or 2025: we tested scaling a chemistry model's annual mean against the satellite anchor and found it anti-correlated with the true year-to-year signal (it misses the COVID-2021 and fuel-crisis-2022 emission anomalies). Rather than publish a confidently wrong number, the explorer ends where the observational anchor ends. When the satellite product is extended, the explorer will be too.

Uncertainty

Every field carries a calibrated 90% interval (toggle "90% upper bound" on the map; every point-query and health readout shows the interval). The intervals come from conformal calibration over 2019–2023 — the same years the explorer serves — so they are in-regime by construction. Health figures are always shown with their 95% confidence interval; the central death estimate should never be cited alone.

Documented episodes

Three independently documented Kandy haze events are provided as one-click presets. The model reconstructs unhealthy levels on all three with the right mechanism (high transboundary background under a calm, shallow boundary layer): the November 2019 transboundary spike, the December 2022 nationwide haze (NBRO advisory), and the February–March 2023 inter-monsoon haze (CEA advisory).
